    The motivation for this project stems from the need to enhance food distribution efficiency by analyzing consumption patterns, production capacities, and various factors affecting the supply chain. By identifying regions with surplus and deficit food supplies, the project aims to develop optimization models that consider export costs, distances, and sustainability factors. The goal is to provide actionable recommendations for improving food distribution systems, ensuring long-term sustainability, and addressing global food security challenges. Continuous monitoring and evaluation will allow for iterative refinement and optimization of distribution strategies to maximize resource utilization.
